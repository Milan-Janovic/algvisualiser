We will use integer representation further on, but operations like XORs or shifts are done in bitwise manner.
Firstly, we split key K into left KL and right KR part.
In case of 256 bit key KL is equal to left 128 bits of K and KR is equal to right 128 bits of K. In case of 192 bit key KL is equal to left 128 bits of K and KR is equal to right 64 bits + negation of right 64 bits of K.
This is done to ensure compatibility of 192 and 256 bit keys
Then we XOR KL with KR. And split KL to left KLL and right KLR part.
KLL = {{KLL1}}
KRL = {{KLR1}}
Then we put KLL through F-function in 2 iterations with constants0-1 and after every F-function we XOR it's result with KLR and swap KLL and KLR. Next we XOR concatenated KLL and KLR with KL from begining of this process and split newly obtained KL back to KLL and KLR. Lastly we repeat 2 iterations long process of putting KLL through F-function with constants2-4 and after every F-function we XOR it's result with KLR and swap KLL and KLR. Ka is obtained as concatenation of KLL and KLR
Ka = {{Ka}}
This process is very similiar to Ka key generation for 128 bit key. For further reference please see 128 bit key Ka generation
Kb key is generated in very simial fashion. Firstly we XOR Ka and KR and we split this result (KR) to KRL and KRR

Then we put KRL through F-function in 2 iterations with constants5-6 and after every F-function we XOR it's result with KRR and swap KRL and KRR. After that we concatenate KRL and KRR to obtain Kb.

Generally, decryption process is encryption process in reverse manner.
First we split cipher text CT into left CTL and right CTR part.
Then we XOR PTL with kw3 and PTR with kw4.
CT = {{CT_final}}
Next we repeat the encryption process in reverse order so we put PTL throught F-function 24 times with kl24-1 subkeys, each time we XOR the result of F-function with PTR18-1.
Then we swap PTL and PTR, same way we did in case of Ka key generation.
Remember that after every six iterations of putting PTL throught F-function, XOR-ing the result with PTR and swaping PTL and PTR we put PTL through FL-function and PTR through FL-1-function.
